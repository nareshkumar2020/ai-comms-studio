# User Research & Prototype Vision

## Context & Problem Space
Internal communications teams are expected to act as high-velocity publishing houses, especially following innovation events. They must parse technical or strategic information and instantly distribute it across multiple formats—from LinkedIn posts and executive briefings to internal web articles and email recaps. 

Currently, the workflow is fundamentally broken: drafting is manual, tailoring content to specific channels takes too much time, and brand consistency degrades when multiple team members are rushing to meet deadlines.

## Key Challenges
1.  **Channel Fragmentation:** Translating a single keynote speech into an engaging 200-word LinkedIn post, a comprehensive internal blog, and a bulleted executive summary requires completely different cognitive context shifts.
2.  **The "Blank Page" Bottleneck:** Synthesizing raw event transcripts, panel takeaways, or messy notes into a coherent first draft is the most time-consuming step of the process.
3.  **Inconsistent Quality & Tone:** With tight deadlines, writers often stray from brand guidelines. Content either becomes too technical or too fluffy.
4.  **Review Gridlock:** Manual reviews for compliance, tone, and formatting lead to endless iteration cycles, delaying critical announcements.

## Ideal AI Use Cases
*   **Structured Transformation:** Turning unstructured data (messy notes, transcripts) into structured content (drafts).
*   **Variant Generation:** Automatically generating channel-specific variations (e.g., social media vs. internal newsletter) from a single core message.
*   **Automated Guardrails:** Acting as a "first pass" editor that flags tone deviations, missing data points, or compliance risks before a human reviewer ever sees it.
